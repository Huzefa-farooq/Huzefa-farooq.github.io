Projects

Force-Sensor Surgical Instrument

Situation

Minimally invasive robotic surgery lacks reliable force feedback, leading to unintentional tissue damage. Our research lab aimed to design a low-cost sensing solution for integration into surgical tools.

Task

My role was to design and prototype a capacitive force sensing system with the required mechanical, electrical, and signal processing components, while maintaining accuracy and compactness.

Action

Designed the capacitive sensor structure, created PCB and DAQ electronics using ESP-based hardware, and integrated AD7746/ADS1115 for precision sensing. Built calibration workflows and tested performance under different loading conditions. Also modelled behaviour and ensured mechanical integration into a surgical tool.

Result

Achieved less than eight percent error in normal and shear force measurements. Delivered a functional prototype with real-time wireless data acquisition. The project received recognition for innovation and contributed to further lab development.
